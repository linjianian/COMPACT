# COMPACT Mouse Surgery Procedure

## Part I. Viral Injection & Head Bar Fixation

### 1. Syringe Assembly and Viral Loading

![Figure 1. Syringe assembly and viral loading setup](figures/fig_01_syringe_assembly.png)

1. Secure the injector onto the working stage using **three pieces of tape**.
2. Prepare the pre-made needle tip (broken needle) and fill it with oil.
3. Attach the front pad and small tunnel to the injector.
4. Mount the needle tip onto the needle holder.
5. Insert the assembled holder into the injector and rotate to lock it in place.
6. Using a pipette tip, collect **up to ~4 µL** of viral solution.
7. Remove the pipette tip from the pipette and secure it onto the movable metal stage using adhesive putty.
8. Under the microscope, carefully insert the needle tip into the pipette tip.
9. Slowly aspirate the viral solution:
   - Aspirate for ~10 seconds  
   - Pause briefly  
   - Continue aspiration

> **Critical:** Ensure the liquid meniscus is **not aspirated into air**.

10. Once all viral solution has been aspirated, gently withdraw the pipette tip from the needle.
11. Place the injector aside and secure it until use.

---

### 2. Mouse Anesthesia

![Figure 2. Mouse anesthesia and positioning](figures/fig_02_mouse_anesthesia.png)

- After anesthesia, gently pinch the tail or paw to confirm adequate anesthetic depth.
- Moisten fur with ethanol (EtOH).

---

### 3. Clear the Skull

![Figure 3. Skull exposure with bregma and lambda](figures/fig_03_skull_exposure.png)

1. Shave the hair.
2. Apply iodine to disinfect the scalp.
3. Clean with ethanol (EtOH) in circular motions (center outward).
4. Repeat iodine + EtOH **three times**.
5. Remove the scalp using scissors or a scalpel.
   - Ensure **bregma** and **lambda** are fully exposed.
6. Use **3M Vetbond Adhesive** to glue scalp edges to the exposed skull.

---

### 4. Mouse Anchoring

![Figure 4. Mouse anchoring using ear bars and tooth bar](figures/fig_04_mouse_anchoring.png)

1. Place a heating pad under the mouse and connect it to a temperature controller.
2. Position **two auxiliary ear bars** at pits in front of the ears.
3. Secure the mouse with a tooth bar connected to the anesthesia inhalation mask.
   - Hook the upper incisors properly.

---

### 5. Calibration

![Figure 5. Calibration at bregma and lambda](figures/fig_05_calibration.png)

1. Mount the manipulation arm with the injector onto the surgery platform.
2. Point the needle tip to **Bregma**:
   - Record DV, ML, AP coordinates  
   - Set Bregma as (0, 0, 0)
3. Move to **Lambda** and record DV.
4. Adjust tooth bar so DV(Bregma) ≈ DV(Lambda) (< 0.02 mm).
5. Mark viral injection coordinates on the skull.

---

### 6. Drilling

![Figure 6. Skull drilling at injection sites](figures/fig_06_drilling.png)

1. Raise the manipulation arm to avoid needle damage.
2. Drill using a **0.5 mm drill bit** at marked sites.
   - Sterilize drill using a heated glass bead sterilizer.
3. Equipment examples:
   - Stoelting Micromotor High-Speed Drill (51449)
   - FST Burrs for Micro Drill (19007-05)
4. Confirm drilling:
   - Lower injector to check if it passes
   - If not, pre-penetrate bone with a thicker needle

---

### 7. Viral Injection

![Figure 7. Viral injection procedure](figures/fig_07_viral_injection.png)

1. Lower injector to the drilled site.
2. Lower to target depth.
3. Inject at **1 nL/s for ~10 minutes**.
4. Allow **5 minutes** recovery.
5. Slowly retract injector.
6. Repeat for additional sites if needed.
7. Fully remove the injector.

---

### 8. Mount the Head Bar

![Figure 8. Head bar alignment and mounting](figures/fig_08_headbar_mounting.png)

1. Disinfect head bar and actuator.
2. Mount to mechanical arm and position above skull.
3. Align:
   - Central axis with skull center
   - Horizontal axis with future COMPACT insertion site

---

### 9. Head Bar Positioning

![Figure 9. Head bar positioning and screw sites](figures/fig_09_headbar_positioning.png)

1. Lower head bar onto skull.
2. Mark **two screw locations**:
   - One anterior  
   - One posterior
3. Avoid major blood vessels.

---

### 10. Screw Placement

![Figure 10. Screw placement for head bar fixation](figures/fig_10_screw_placement.png)

1. Drill holes using a **0.7 mm drill bit**.
2. Insert screws (~1.6 mm length):
   - Short screw (anterior)
   - Slightly longer screw (posterior)

---

### 11. Lower Head Bar Assembly

![Figure 11. Lowering head bar and gap filling](figures/fig_11_headbar_lowering.png)

1. Lower actuator + head bar onto skull.
2. Head bar curvature should match skull surface.
3. Inspect side fit.
4. Fill gaps using **cyanoacrylate (Krazy Blue)** if necessary.

---

### 12. Head Bar Fixation

![Figure 12. Head bar fixation with dental cement](figures/fig_12_headbar_fixation.png)

1. Apply Krazy Blue around head bar perimeter.
2. Apply **dental cement (Contemporary Ortho-Jet, Lang Dental)**:
   - Ensure sufficient viscosity  
   - Cover head bar and screws

---

### 13. Seal Center Opening

![Figure 13. Sealing center opening of head bar](figures/fig_13_center_seal.png)

- Apply a **very thin layer** of Krazy Blue around the center opening.
- Allow to dry completely.

---

### 14. Remove Actuator Assembly

![Figure 14. Actuator removal](figures/fig_14_actuator_removal.png)

- Carefully demount column + head bar from actuator.
- Remove column screws.

---

### 15. Recovery Period

![Figure 15. Post-surgical recovery](figures/fig_15_recovery.png)

- Allow recovery for **2–3 days to 1 week**, depending on viral expression needs.

---

## Part II. COMPACT Insertion

### Preparation

![Figure 16. COMPACT insertion preparation](figures/fig_16_compact_prep.png)

1. Maintain anesthesia with inhalation mask.
2. Fix head bar to stage using side disks.
3. Disinfect scalp with iodine and ethanol (×3).
4. Draw a **circle** around injection site.
5. Draw **eight directional lines** to preserve orientation.

---

### Skull Opening

![Figure 17. Skull opening for COMPACT insertion](figures/fig_17_skull_opening.png)

- Drill circle using a **0.5 mm dental drill**.
- Do **not** drill fully through.
- Use a >30G needle to remove remaining bone membrane.

---

### Method 1: Vacuum Pump & Open-Ended Capillary

![Figure 18. Vacuum-assisted tissue removal](figures/fig_18_vacuum_capillary.png)

1. Mount 15 µm thin-walled quartz capillary on metal rod.
2. Align capillary with insertion site using eight reference lines.
3. Insert at **10 µm/s**.
4. Remove tissue using a **24G right-angle needle** connected to vacuum pump:
   - Vacuum → move → repeat  
   - Do not vacuum too long at final depth
5. Turn off vacuum and retract pump.

> **Note:** After reaching final depth, do not dwell to avoid tissue damage.

---

### Method 2: Sharp-Pointed Needle

![Figure 19. Sharp-pointed needle insertion](figures/fig_19_sharp_needle.png)

1. Mount sharp-pointed needle.
2. Align with insertion site.
3. Insert at **10 µm/s**.
4. Wait ~5 minutes.
5. Withdraw slowly at **~10 µm/s**.

---

### Actuator Assembly

![Figure 20. Actuator and capillary assembly](figures/fig_20_actuator_assembly.png)

1. Components:
   - Piston rod  
   - Borosilicate glass tube (ID: 0.81 mm)
2. Insert lightly moistened cotton tip into tube.
3. Wet tube interior with water (surface tension coupling).
4. Insert closed-ended 15 µm capillary (UV-sealed tip).
5. Mount assembly onto stage.

---

### Capillary Insertion

![Figure 21. Closed-ended capillary insertion](figures/fig_21_capillary_insertion.png)

1. Align piston-capillary with previous insertion site.
2. Insert closed-ended capillary at **10 µm/s**.
3. Apply **KWIK-SIL (WPI)** silicone adhesive.
4. Cover silicone with dental cement.

---

### Finalization

![Figure 22. Finalized COMPACT implantation](figures/fig_22_finalization.png)

1. Raise tube and piston carefully, leaving capillary in place.
2. Fill remaining head bar gaps with dental cement.
3. Attach artificial cap.
4. Recovery with cap for **2–4 weeks**.

---

## Part III. COMPACT Surgery Equipment

### GRIN Lens & Prism Attachment

![Figure 23. GRIN lens and prism attachment](figures/fig_23_grin_prism.png)

- Apply **very thin UV epoxy** to GRIN lens.
- Place prism using tweezers.
- Allow epoxy to self-spread.

> **Important:** Attach prism **only after** GRIN lens is mounted on imaging probe.

---

### Closed-Ended Capillary Preparation

![Figure 24. Closed-ended capillary preparation](figures/fig_24_closed_capillary_prep.png)

1. Spread thin epoxy layer on stage.
2. Touch 15 µm capillary vertically into epoxy.
3. UV cure epoxy.
4. Incubate in heater for ~12 hours.
5. UV disinfect for **25–30 minutes** before use.

**Resulting epoxy layer:** ~0.2–0.3 mm

---

## Part IV. Closed-Ended Capillary Design

### Method 1: Thin UV Epoxy Seal

![Figure 25. UV epoxy–sealed capillary tip](figures/fig_25_uv_epoxy_tip.png)

- Same procedure as above.
- Final epoxy thickness: **0.2–0.3 mm**

### Method 2: Glass Disc Seal

![Figure 26. Glass disc capillary seal](figures/fig_26_glass_disc_tip.png)

1. Place glass disc (OD < capillary ID) on stage.
2. Apply minimal epoxy to both sides.
3. Align using vertical + horizontal cameras.
4. Lower capillary onto disc.

**Final epoxy thickness:** ~0.1–0.2 mm

---

## Notes

- Handle glass capillaries with extreme care.
- Maintain sterility throughout all procedures.
- Follow institutional animal care and use guidelines at all times.
